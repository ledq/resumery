# What "good" means: the quality rubric

Nine dimensions. **Truthfulness (9) is the gate, never traded; 1-8 are maximized within it.**
A dimension at or above bar is done; do not chase a "tighter" version past it (that is what
makes the loop never end).

Content
1. **Coverage**: surfaces bank evidence for the JD's real requirements.
2. **Surfacing**: uses the strongest *true* material the bank holds, not weaker stand-ins.
3. **Prioritization**: most JD-relevant first, weak/irrelevant content cut; built for a
   recruiter's seconds-long scan. Order is quality.
4. **Credibility**: every bullet proves an outcome with real specifics (result + how +
   measure), not just stated activity.

Craft
5. **Bullet craft**: strong action verb; one story per bullet; lands on a decision/outcome,
   not a feature list; not too long.
6. **Coherence**: consistent positioning and voice for THIS role across bullets and skills order.

Presentation
7. **Layout**: one page, clean, scannable (owned by `ops/render.py` + the hook).
8. **ATS**: machine-readable; the JD's real keywords present where the bank supports them.

Gate
9. **Truthfulness**: every claim traces to the bank. Bounds 1-4; never relaxed to improve them.

## House style (non-negotiable)

- No em dashes. Semicolons for linked ideas, commas for asides.
- No "~" for approximation; write "approximately" or "about", or state the number.
- No filler: "passionate about", "leveraged", "spearheaded", "utilized", "collaborated with
  cross-functional teams", or any phrase that fits anyone.
- No soft-skill padding; let technical content carry the bullet.
